TAXATION

The services of the Rijksmuseum are exclusively focused on providing (art) historical information about objects that have been manipulated with the museum's collection. In accordance with the ethical code for musics, drawn up by the International Council of Museums (ICOM), the museum does not provide any information about the value of (art) objects. If you want to know the monetary value of an (art) object, there are various options for investigating this:


DO YOUR OWN TAXATION RESEARCH AT THE RIJKSMUSEUM RESEARCH LIBRARY


    • Auction Catalogs
      About 60,000 auction catalogs can be consulted in the Rijksmuseum Research Library. The price lists have been added to the catalogs since 1989. From 2016, the price lists are digitally added to the records in the catalog.

      Databases
      In addition, the following relevant databases can be consulted in the Rijksmuseum Research Library:

    • Artnet.com
      Artnet is a database in which auction results are collected. Of the decorative arts, auction results from 2004 to the present are present. The auction results of Fine Art & Design start from 1980. The Artnet Price Database contains more than 11 million auction results. Descriptions of the auction catalog are available and there is often visual material available.

      Artprice.com
      Contains more than 4 million auction results from 1700 to the present and covers more than 405,000 artists from the 4th century to the present. Treats exclusively visual arts. Image material is available.

    • EXPERTISE OF AUCTION HOUSE OR APPRAISER


    For valuation of (art) objects in your own possession you can turn to an auction house, art dealer or independent appraiser.
