    Take research into your own hands

PERSONAL RESEARCH

If you decide to do your own research, below are listed a few possible ways to gain more information. If you still want to fill out the form to contact the Rijksmuseum Department of Research Services, go back to the homepage.


GATHER (ART) HISTORICAL EXPERTISE


    • Dutch Artists
      If you (presumably) have a work by a Dutch artist in your possession, you can contact the RKD - Netherlands Institute for Art History in The Hague. A work of art can be examined by one of the specialists at the RKD for a fee. The RKD also organizes freely accessible assessment consultation hours.

      Art from the Southern Netherlands
      For information about works produced in the Southern Netherlands (nowadays Belgium) you can contact the Rubenianum in Antwerp. The Rubenianum provides unrestricted and without obligation art-historical opinions about the origin, attribution or iconography of southern Dutch artworks from the 16th and 17th centuries.

    • Archaeological finds and art from antiquity
      For information about archaeological finds you can contact the National Service for Cultural Heritage. Concrete knowledge questions from the field can be answered via the Archeology platform. The National Museum of Antiquities in Leiden holds an archaeological consultation hour by appointment where archaeological finds and objects can be determined.

      Delftware
      For more information about Delftware you can visit the website www.delftsaardewerk.nl / www.dutchdelftware.com. A large part of the Dutch Delftware collection is made available on this website and the website is connected to experts in Delftware that can assess objects.

      Photography
      For more information about photography, see the Dutch Photo Museum, Amsterdam City Archives and the National Archives.

      Asian Art
      For ethnography you can visit the Museum Volkenkunde in Leiden.

      Militaria / Maritime history
      The National Military Museum in Soesterberg has a knowledge and information center in the field of militaria and the armed forces. Maritime Digital is a joint database of a number of (maritime) museums, in which data can be looked up about objects and literature that are located in these museums. The Maritime Portal is a central website where all maritime-historical institutions can be found and accessed.

      Silver
      The website http://www.925-1000.com offers an overview of modern brand practice per country, sometimes with a historical background and gives an overview of the most important literature.

      Arts and crafts / Costumes and accessories
      Victoria & Albert Museum London, Modemuze

      Numismatics
      National Numismatic Collection, Teylers Museum, KB
